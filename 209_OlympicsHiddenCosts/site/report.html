Olympics — The Hidden Costs

By Floriane Leynaud, Hope Tadsen, Bernard Julve — DATASCI 209, Summer 2026

⌄

Every four years, a new city takes on the Olympic bill. Since 1976, fourteen cities across five continents have played host — and each one has walked away with a very different fiscal and social footprint. In this report, we explore the hidden costs of hosting the Olympics, examining the financial and social impacts on host cities and their residents.

H1 — The games and the public weight

The Olympic bill doesn't disappear when the closing ceremony ends. Every host since 1976 is plotted below — each circle sized by the total cost of its Games and coloured by who ended up carrying the debt. Montreal (1976) needed 30 years to pay off its stadium; Athens' (2004) venues still sit on the public books. Hover any city to see its cost, overrun, and debt status.
