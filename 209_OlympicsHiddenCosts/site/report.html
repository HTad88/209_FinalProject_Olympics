Olympics — The Hidden Costs

By Floriane Leynaud, Hope Tadsen, Bernard Julve — DATASCI 209, Summer 2026

⌄

Every four years, a new city takes on the Olympic bill. Since 1976, fourteen cities across five continents have played host — and each one has walked away with a very different fiscal and social footprint. In this report, we explore the hidden costs of hosting the Olympics, examining the financial and social impacts on host cities and their residents.

H1 — Host cities carry the debt for decades

The Olympic bill doesn't disappear when the closing ceremony ends. Every host since 1976 is plotted below — each circle sized by its sports-related cost (venues and operations, in 2022 US$) and coloured by how long the host carried the debt. Montreal (1976) needed 30 years to pay off its stadium; Athens' (2004) venues still sit on the public books. Use the filter to compare Summer and Winter Games, and hover any city for its cost, overrun and debt outcome.
