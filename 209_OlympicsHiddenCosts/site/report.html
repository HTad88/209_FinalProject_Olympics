Olympics - The hidden costs /

By Floriane Leynaud, Hope Tadsen, Bernard Julve — DATASCI 209, Summer 2026

Every four years, a new city takes on the Olympic bill. Since 1976, fourteen cities across five continents have played host — and each one has walked away with a very different fiscal and social footprint. From Montreal to Athens, from Atlanta to Beijing, the Games have left a trail of debt, dislocation, and disappointment. In this report, we explore the hidden costs of hosting the Olympics, examining the financial and social impacts on host cities and their residents.
